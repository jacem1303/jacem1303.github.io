About

A little more about me

How I got here

I grew up in Gilbert, Arizona, and I'm now at BYU finishing my finance degree — I already knocked out an associate's in business at UVU on the way there. Real estate is what pulled me into finance. The summer I spent as an acquisitions intern at a "We Buy Ugly Houses" franchise pretty much sealed it: I'd run the numbers on a house in the morning and then go tear out drywall on it that same afternoon. Seeing both the spreadsheet and the job site changed how I look at a deal. A model is only as good as what's actually happening on the ground.

These days I'm most into real estate private equity and investing, and I did Eastdil's real estate finance program to get a real feel for how the bigger firms think about a deal. Outside of class, running No Pane Cleaning keeps me honest about the unglamorous side of business — chasing down customers, scheduling crews, and making sure we actually get paid.

What I'm after

I want an internship where I can underwrite deals, build models, and learn from people who are flat-out better than me at this. I'd rather show up over-prepared than wing it, I move fast, and I try to do things the right way even when nobody's checking.
