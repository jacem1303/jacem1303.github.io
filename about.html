About

A bit more about me

Background

I grew up in Gilbert, Arizona, and I'm now studying finance at the BYU Marriott School of Business, with an Associate of Science in Business Management from Utah Valley University already behind me. My path into finance runs through real estate: I spent a summer as an acquisitions analyst at a HomeVestors "We Buy Ugly Houses" franchise, underwriting deals in the morning and doing the hands-on rehab work in the afternoon. Seeing both sides — the spreadsheet and the job site — changed how I think about a deal.

I'm drawn to real estate private equity and investment analysis, and I completed the Eastdil 2026 Real Estate Finance Training Program to build out that foundation. Alongside school I co-founded No Pane Cleaning, which keeps me sharp on the parts of business that don't show up in a model: sales, scheduling, and actually getting paid.

What I'm looking for

I'm seeking internship and analyst roles in real estate finance and investments where I can underwrite, build models, and learn from people who do this at a high level. I work efficiently, I'd rather over-prepare than wing it, and I care about doing things with integrity.
