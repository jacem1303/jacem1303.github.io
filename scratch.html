Why I'm Hooked on Golf

Four-plus hours outside, one little ball, and a game you can never quite beat. I picked it up a few years back and now it's the thing I think about when I should be thinking about other things. Here's the short version of why — with a little volleyball thrown in, since that's where I came from.

How I Actually Practice

Most people think golf is just hitting a ball. It's really four or five separate skills stacked on top of each other, and you're only as good as your weakest one. Here's how I rank what to work on — and the specific stuff I drill inside each (an ordered list of priorities, with an unordered list nested under each one):

  1. Tee shots — they set the tone for the hole
    • Aim at something smaller than the fairway
    • Smooth tempo beats trying to kill it
    • Commit before I ever stand over the ball
  2. Irons & approach — this is how you get birdie looks
    • Know my real carry numbers, not my hero numbers
    • Aim for the fat part of the green, not the pin
  3. Short game — where scores actually drop
    • Chip to a landing spot, not the hole
    • Grind on the 3-to-15-footers that decide rounds
  4. The mental game — the hardest six inches in golf
    • Reset after a bad shot; the next one doesn't know about it
    • Play the smart shot, not the highlight-reel one

Funny enough, the mental reset is the part I'm best at — and that came from volleyball, not golf. I played on an Arizona state and national championship team in 2021, and the biggest thing it taught me was how to forget the last point fast. Turns out that works just as well after a shanked 7-iron.

The Greatest Shot I've Ever Seen

Tiger Woods, 16th hole, final round of the 2005 Masters. He chips from way left of the green, and the ball tracks down the slope, slows to a crawl, and stops dead on the lip of the cup — the Nike swoosh sitting there for a beat like it's posing for the camera — and then drops.
