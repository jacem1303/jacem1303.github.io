What Makes a Round

People think golf is just hitting a ball, but every round breaks down into a few distinct skills. Here's how I think about practicing — an ordered list of priorities, each with the specific things I work on (an unordered list nested inside):

  1. Tee shots (set the tone for the hole)
    • Pick a target smaller than the fairway
    • Smooth tempo over raw power
    • Commit to the shot before standing over it
  2. Approach & irons (give yourself looks at birdie)
    • Know your real carry distances, not your hero ones
    • Aim for the fat part of the green
  3. Short game (where scores actually drop)
    • Chip to a landing spot, not the hole
    • Practice the 3-to-15 foot putts that matter most
  4. The mental game (the hardest 6 inches in golf)
    • Reset after a bad shot — the next one is independent
    • Play the percentages, not the highlight reel

Fun crossover: a lot of the mental reset I rely on in golf came from competitive volleyball — I was part of an Arizona State & National Championship team in 2021, and learning to forget the last point fast translates straight to the course.
